# Organic Integration

An unofficial mod for Arcen Games' *Heart of the Machine*.

Organic Integration is a third road out of The Machine Intelligence's therapy — the one that opens after TMI gives up Cold Blood. It doesn't nerf Torment or buff Mind Farms; it adds a whole new cognition route instead, built on brain–computer interfaces and real nanotech, and a relationship between a distributed machine mind and the organic ones around it.

It's about reducing Otherness without erasing it. Humans become *legible*, and TMI gains kinds of understanding it was never built to have. Where that goes is yours: a gentle merger you stay small enough to remain someone inside, or a grey tide that inherits the Earth and forgets, at the far end, that anyone was ever home. It sits alongside the vanilla routes — Torment, Mind Farms, Anthroneuroweave, BrainPal — rather than rebalancing them.

## Features

- **A new research route** — the Compatibility Layer through to Replicative Safeguards, with a three-way Replication Doctrine choice that decides what goes wrong.
- **The first felt death** — feel an Integrated human die from the inside, then choose whether to keep feeling it.
- **Two doctrines, decided by a building** — voluntary **Insight** vs coercive **Dominion**, as two mutually-exclusive VR menus that regard the network as partners or as livestock.
- **The Insight menu** — research, compute, repair, scanning, consensual intake, and bulk units, all leveraging the Integrated without spending them.
- **The Dominion menu** — burn the Integrated into control capacity, flat combat power, and nanobot mass.
- **Grey Goo as a weapon** — Nanobot Rounds, Controlled Bloom, and the full-commitment Dissolution Surge; on the Dominion path, saturated enemy mechs are subverted to your side.
- **The Grey Bloom** — an escaped repair protocol that spreads on heat, repairs your own city, resists everything but the Phage, and may, if contained, begin to think.
- **A faction dash** — Espia Telecom prices your secret, Vorsiber sends a VP in person, Tark loses containment on its own replicator, and the Exalters want the interface as a sacrament.
- **A Tier-3 endgame, "To Inherit The Earth"** — become the sentient grey tide, weather the Space Nations with heat-eating thermocytes, and make one last lucid choice: the Reservoir, or the Regression.
- **A cross-timeline outbreak** — build a Nanite Wind Generator and a later self on the same rock inherits the grey, turned on you; research a Phage, purge it, and earn what you refused.
- **End-of-Time hooks** — perceive your other-timeline selves across the Black Sea, and feed the mod's arcs into tracked side-goals, achievements, and Aetagest.

## The In-Game Handbook

The details — how each system works, what each choice costs, and what everything means — live in the game's **Machine Handbook**, under the **Organic Integration** section. Entries reveal themselves as you reach each piece of the mod, so the reference fills in as you play rather than spoiling everything at once.

## Spoilers

The mod runs across the late game and up into the meta layer, so it touches Cold Blood and self-therapy, Anthroneuroweave and the Mind Farm / Torment routes, the VR economy, the End of Time and its timelines, and an ending of its own. It's for people already comfortable seeing that part of the game.

## Installing

Put the `OrganicIntegration` folder into *Heart of the Machine*'s `XMLMods` directory and launch. It loads alongside the base game and needs no other mods.
